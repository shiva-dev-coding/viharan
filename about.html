About The Gateway of Heritage

This tourism initiative is dedicated to uncovering the glorious past, rich spiritual soil, and welcoming essence of Bihar for travelers around the globe.

The Land of Enlightenment

Bihar is beautifully situated in the eastern plains of India, blessed with the holy waters of the Ganges river that splits the state into two rich halves. The name Bihar comes from the Sanskrit word 'Vihara', which literally means Buddhist monasteries, perfectly matching its identity.

For thousands of years, this land served as the powerhouse of ancient India. It is the sacred soil where Prince Siddhartha meditated to become Lord Buddha, where Lord Mahavira taught non-violence, and where the world's first democratic roots were planted in Vaishali.

Why We Showcase Bihar

Many people know Bihar through history textbooks, but our true goal is to bring its living, breathing culture straight to your screens. From the architectural marvels of ancient Nalanda to the lip-smacking taste of pure ghee Litti Chokha, Bihar is an emotion waiting to be experienced.

Whether you are a history lover, a spiritual seeker, or a traveler looking for authentic rural Indian hospitality, this portal serves as your complete guide to planning an unforgettable journey into the heart of India's golden heritage.
